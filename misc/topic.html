Development of VR Technology in Video Games

What is VR?

VR is currently split into 3 different categories. Non-immersive VR, semi-immersive VR and Fully immersive VR.

Non-Immersive VR

First you have your classic non immersive VR experience which is just your typical gaming set up of your keyboard or controller and such with your monitor or TV in front of you. Your standard gaming experience.

Semi-Immersive VR

Then we have semi immersive VR which is the current stage of VR which uses either large monitors to surround the player or a headset that sits on the players head. This lets the player get immersed into their gaming environment visually, but not physically.

Fully-Immersive VR

Finally we have fully immersive VR which is the future of VR gaming and is currently being worked on as we speak. At this stage they are continuing the use of headsets but are trying to achieve real virtual 3D views in order to bring the virtual items right in front of you to be able to interact with. This also includes the use of body controls to bring yourself into your gaming environment. The idea is for you to not be able to tell real life from virtual life.
